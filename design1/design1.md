Design 1 upgrades the renderer's shading quality from flat, blocky terrain to smooth, continuously-lit surfaces at zero additional BRAM cost. The core change is in normal.sv, which previously read four heightmap neighbours in a "plus" pattern to compute a central-difference surface normal. It now reads the four cells of the enclosing grid square — (ix,iy), (ix+1,iy), (ix,iy+1), (ix+1,iy+1) — and computes the normal with forward differences instead. The same four reads are reused to bilinearly interpolate the exact surface height at the ray's sub-cell hit position, producing a new h_interp output that drives the shader's altitude colouring. To support this, the hit position (Px_hit, Py_hit) is now propagated through march_step.sv and marcher.sv so the fractional position within the hit cell is known. The bilinear interpolation is implemented as three lerps split across two pipeline stages, adding one cycle of latency to normal.sv (4→5 stages) and lifting total render latency from 77 to 78 cycles. The marcher itself is unchanged: hit detection is still nearest-neighbour, so silhouette edges remain on cell boundaries while the lit surface and colour ramp become smooth. The design retains 1 pixel/cycle throughput and the full 20-BRAM footprint (16 marcher ports + 4 normal ports), keeping the external ray_unit interface byte-for-byte identical to the base version. It therefore drops into the existing Vivado block design with no rewiring, requiring only that RENDER_LATENCY in the top wrapper be updated to 78. End-to-end simulation against the frozen interface confirms correct RGB output, smooth shading gradients, and the measured 78-cycle latency.
